7 Days in Rajasthan: The Complete Photographer's Route

Back to Guides
December 10, 2023 12 min read Rajasthan, India

Rajasthan is not a destination you plan — it is one that happens to you. I came for the forts and left with sand in my shoes, saffron on my fingers, and more photographs than I knew what to do with. This is the route I would take again without changing a single day.

Getting there

The easiest entry point is Jaipur — well connected to Delhi by both flight (1 hour) and train (4.5 hours on the Shatabdi Express). I flew in from Sofia via Istanbul, with a one-night stopover in Delhi.

💡 Tip Book trains on the IRCTC website at least 2 weeks in advance. Tourist quota seats are available even when regular seats are full — look for the "TQ" option.

Where to stay

Rajasthan's heritage havelis — converted merchant mansions — are some of the most atmospheric places to sleep anywhere in the world. In Jaipur, look for properties in the old city walls. In Jodhpur, a rooftop view of the blue city is non-negotiable.

What to photograph

The golden hour at Mehrangarh Fort in Jodhpur is unmissable — arrive 30 minutes before sunset and find a spot on the northern ramparts. In Jaipur, the Hawa Mahal facade catches the morning light beautifully between 7 and 9am.

📸 Photography tip The blue houses of Jodhpur photograph best from above — hire a guide to take you to the rooftop terraces of the old city. Early morning before 8am gives you the streets without tourists.

Best time to visit

October to March is the ideal window — cool mornings, manageable midday heat, and the golden light that makes every photograph look painted. Avoid May and June (extreme heat above 45°C) and July–September (monsoon, though it brings dramatic skies).

Practical notes

India requires a visa in advance — the e-visa (evisa.india.gov.in) takes 3–5 days and costs $25. The rupee is the local currency; carry cash for smaller towns. SIM cards with data are cheap and available at the airport.

💰 Budget guide Mid-range: €50–80/day including a heritage hotel, all meals, and entry fees. Budget: €20–30/day in guesthouses with street food. Entrance fees to major forts: €5–10 each.
