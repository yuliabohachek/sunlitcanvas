What a kit is for

A trip. A person. A season of your life.

Anything that deserves more than a phone folder. A journey that changed you, someone you no longer see every day, a chapter that is closing — whatever carries weight.

A journey
Solo trip, family holiday, weekend away. The place that changed you — distilled to its ten essential moments.
A person or a time
A grandmother's kitchen. A friendship across years. A parent before they were ill. The version of someone you want to keep.
A season of life
A child's first year. A move, a beginning, an ending. Anything that felt important while it was happening.

How it works

Four steps. One object.

01
You send everything
Share your folder — all the photos, unedited, in any order. Answer five short questions about what this memory means to you.
02
I find the ten frames
One surprise, one face, one detail, one transition, one quiet second. Your memory reduced to what it actually is.
03
I make the object
A hand-stitched zine, a keepsake card, a kraft envelope. Your full gallery goes into a private archive linked by QR inside the zine.
04
It arrives in 7 days
Delivered anywhere in Europe. The reveal happens when you open the envelope — not in a preview, not before. That is part of the object.

Before I begin

Five questions.
No brief needed.

You don't need to know which photos you want. That's my job. I only need to understand what this memory feels like — so the ten frames I choose feel true, not just well-composed.

  • 1What would you most want to remember about this, ten years from now?
  • 2Was there a moment that surprised you?
  • 3Is there something important that isn't in any photo?
  • 4One word or feeling that describes the whole thing.
  • 5Who is this kit for — you, or someone else?
